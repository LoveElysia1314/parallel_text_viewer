# Volume 1, Prologue: The Proud Princess and the Lazy Neighbor

Taiwanese edition, reprinted from Angel Anime Forum

Angel Anime Input Team

Image source: Ikoku Doku

Input by: kid

Seirei Academy, a private institution. 

From past to present, numerous alumni have gone on to excel in politics and business, boasting one of Japan's highest standards of student excellence. It's a comprehensive school offering seamless progression from junior high through high school and university. With a long history, it has long been the preferred choice for the offspring of both old and new aristocratic families, a prestigious school steeped in tradition.

Students stroll along tree-lined paths toward this traditional campus. 

They chat animatedly with friends or classmates as they make their way to the school buildings. But the moment a female student steps through the school gates, the atmosphere shifts dramatically. 

Those who catch sight of her simultaneously express astonishment and admiration, their gazes following her every move. 

"Wow, who's that girl? She's absolutely stunning!" 

"Don't you know? She was the one who delivered the welcoming speech at last year's entrance ceremony! She's Maria's younger sister." 

"I only saw her from afar back then... Oh my goodness! Up close, she's practically an elf!" 

"That's so true. Even though I'm the same gender and a year older, I can't help but be captivated by her beauty." 

A native Japanese person simply couldn't possess skin so pale it seems translucent, nor eyes so delicately slanted, radiating a sapphire-like brilliance. 

And her silver hair, styled in a princess braid, gleams brilliantly in the morning sunlight. 

Her features, deeply inherited from her Russian father, subtly blend with the gentle, feminine charm passed down from her Japanese mother. 

An unparalleled appearance, combined with a height above average for a girl, slender limbs, and a perfectly proportioned figure—truly embodying the ideal form every woman dreams of. 

Her unique coloring lends her beauty an otherworldly quality. Her name is Alisa Mikhailovna Kujo. A third-year middle schooler last year, she transferred to Seirei Academy and has since topped her grade in every exam, excels in all sports, and has served as the Student Council's treasurer since this year. Truly a prodigy worthy of being called a perfect superhuman. 

"Hey, look over there." 

"Huh? Whoa! It's Kujo-san! Such good luck this early in the morning!" 

"Hey, why don't you go say hello to her?" 

"No way! I'd never dare!" 

"Come on, this isn't like you at all—you usually hit on any pretty girl you see! It's just a simple greeting; are you really scared?" 

"You idiot! Her level... no, her dimension is completely different! If that's how you feel, then you go talk to her yourself!" 

"No way! I don't want to embarrass myself and get targeted by other guys." 

Both boys and girls around her cast envious glances her way. Naturally, everyone slows down and moves aside, allowing her to proceed with effortless grace. 

At this moment, a male student approaches her. As soon as the surrounding students catch sight of him, murmurs erupt. 

"Hi, good morning. What a refreshing morning, isn't it?" 

The male student speaks with a bright smile, and Alisa, without stopping, glances briefly at him, confirms he's a senior by his tie color, and gives a slight nod in acknowledgment. 

"Good morning, senior." 

"Ah, good morning. Is this our first meeting? I'm Ando, a second-year student, and a classmate of your sister." 

"I see." 

The male student who introduced himself as Ando has dyed brown hair, his uniform slightly disheveled, with a silver accessory peeking out from his collar—a modern, stylish, and quite handsome young man. Yet Alisa responds with cold indifference. 

While the male student's charming smile elicits shy squeals from the girls around her, Alisa remains utterly unflappable, responding with complete composure. 

"I've heard your sister talk about you often... I've been wanting to meet you for a long time. How about it? Would you like to have lunch together during the break?" 

"No, thank you." 

She answers immediately, without the slightest hesitation. Faced with such a frosty response, Ando offers a wry smile. 

"Haha... so cold. Well then, at least let's exchange contact information? I'd like to get to know you better." 

"I'm sorry, but I'm not interested in you. If you're only contacting me for this reason, then I'll take my leave now. Oh, and—" 

At this moment, Alisa directs her gaze toward Ando, extending her finger toward his neck. Faced with her cold, piercing eyes and her slender finger pointing directly at him, Ando involuntarily loses his smile, widening his eyes and leaning back slightly. 

"...That violates the school rules." 

Ignoring Ando's fluster, Alisa coldly points out the silver accessory around his neck and issues a stern warning, then simply says, "Excuse me," and quickly walks away. This scene once again sets off a flurry of whispers among the students who had been watching in stunned silence. 

"Wow, even Ando-senpai, the most popular guy among the girls in the second year, got rejected outright! She really is like a proud princess." 

"What on earth is her ideal standard... is there even a boy worthy of her?" 

"Actually, she's probably not interested in boys at all. It's such a waste, considering how beautiful she is." 

"No, no, she doesn't belong to anyone, which actually puts our minds at ease, doesn't it?" 

"That's right. In terms of being an idol, she's even more of an idol than ordinary idols. I'd have no problem looking at her for a lifetime, even worshipping her." 

"Wait, going that far is pretty extreme. But I can understand how you feel." 

Alisa is completely unaware of this conversation unfolding behind her back. She enters the school building, changes her shoes at the shoe locker, and heads to her classroom. 

The male student she so decisively ignored moments ago is already completely forgotten. 

Because incidents of this nature have become so commonplace, they're not worth deliberately keeping in her memory. 

Being the center of attention or being approached by others is an everyday occurrence for Alisa, and her habit of treating all of this with cool detachment is also part of her daily routine. 

As soon as she opens the classroom door, she's immediately the focus of her classmates' attention. 

This too is a daily morning ritual, so Alisa takes it in stride, heading to her seat in the last row by the window, hanging her bag beside her desk, and casually glancing at the seat to her right. 

Sitting in this seat is a male student whose surname sounds similar to hers, causing them to sit next to each other for over a year. 

Known as one of the two most beautiful girls in the first year of high school, Alisa's neighbor, occupying this coveted seat that many boys envy for over a year—Masachika Kuse. 

"………………" 

He's lying face down on his desk, having decided to sleep soundly from early morning. 

This behavior, utterly unbecoming of a student at a school with such fine traditions, causes Alisa, whose expression has remained unchanged until now, to narrow her eyes slightly. 

"Kuse-kun, good morning." 

"..." 

Even when Alisa greets him, Masachika, using his hands as a pillow and lying face down on his desk, remains completely unresponsive. It seems he's not just lying on his desk, but fast asleep. 

Alisa, ignored completely despite her greeting, narrows her eyes even further, causing her classmates who witness this scene to twitch their cheeks involuntarily. 

"Uh, hey~ Kuse? Wake up~" calls out the male student sitting diagonally in front of Masachika, but before Masachika reacts to this voice and awakens... 

Clang! 

"Gukupu!" 

Suddenly, Masachika's desk swiftly shifts sideways with a loud thud, and Masachika jumps up with a startled cry—Alisa, standing beside him, has forcefully kicked the leg of his desk from the side. 

The surrounding students simultaneously make "Ah~" expressions and turn their heads away. 

Alisa is an outstanding student with excellent grades and impeccable conduct, generally indifferent and uninvolved in matters of good or bad, but the one exception she treats with strictness is her neighbor, the epitome of a mischievous student at this school—a fact well-known throughout the grade. 

Alisa scolds Masachika with a sharply sarcastic tone full of contempt, while Masachika lets it all roll off his back. This scene is seen almost every day, so everyone has grown accustomed to it. 

"Kuse-kun, good morning. Another late-night anime binge?" 

Alisa greets him again with a completely nonchalant expression. Masachika still seems far from fully awake. 

Hearing this voice, Masachika looks up in surprise, shrugs as if realizing something, and scratches his head before returning the greeting: 

"Mo... morning, Alya. Yeah, that's exactly what happened." 

The name "Alya," used by Masachika, is Alisa's nickname in Russia. 

Many students use this nickname privately, but among boys, only Masachika uses it in her presence at this school. 

Whether this is due to Masachika's boldness or Alisa's tolerance, no one around them knows. 

Despite being awakened by a kick while sleeping and being scrutinized by an icy gaze, Masachika remains completely unfazed. 

This composed demeanor draws astonished and admiring glances from others, yet Masachika isn't intentionally putting on a show. The reason is... he's already figured it out.
("What's 'wugupu'? 'Wugupu'? Hehe, what a weird sound!")

Alisa looks down at him with eyes that show no disgust—on the contrary, her whole being is laughing deep within her gaze.

Watching him jump up while making strange noises fills Alisa with immense amusement.

However, Alisa seems completely unaware that her innermost thoughts have been laid bare. She returns to her seat and says in a dumbfounded tone:

"You really never learn, do you? Shortening your sleep just to watch anime—what'll happen when you feel sleepy at school?"

"Well, that's true, but the anime actually finished airing at one in the morning... It was the post-show discussion that dragged on too long."

"Post-show discussion? Oh, you mean those online forums where otaku gather to share their thoughts?"

"Nope. I was on the phone with my otaku friends—about two hours, give or take."

"Are you an idiot?"

Alisa delivers this remark with an utterly disdainful eye roll, and Masachika suddenly gazes into the distance, wearing a vacant smile.

"Haha... an idiot, huh... You're right. To passionately express one's love for a work, regardless of time or place... If someone calls such a person an idiot, maybe they really are..."

"I'm sorry. It seems you're not just any ordinary idiot—you're a hopeless case."

"Today Alya's in great form, as always."

Masachika casually shrugs off Alisa's merciless scolding, treating it as if it were mere banter.

Seeing Masachika's attitude, Alisa shakes her head in helpless resignation, when suddenly the bell rings, signaling that homeroom will begin in three minutes.

Students gradually return to their seats, and Alisa also turns back to face forward, starting to unpack her textbooks and notebooks from her bag and place them on her desk.

Among the students who dutifully wait for the homeroom teacher to arrive, observing the etiquette expected of a prestigious school, Masachika stretches mightily, lets out a huge yawn, and blinks his tearful, hazy eyes.

Alisa, glancing sidelong at this scene, quietly giggles toward the window and murmurs softly in Russian:

【Милашка. (You're so cute.)】

"Huh? Did you say something?"

"Nope. I just said, 'That's really unsightly.'"

She responds with a completely impassive expression to Masachika, who heard her soft whisper with remarkable clarity. Upon hearing Alisa's evasive reply, Masachika assumes she's referring to his earlier yawn and responds with a conciliatory "My apologies," this time covering his mouth as he yawns again.

Seeing Masachika like this, Alisa raises one eyebrow in a dismissive manner and giggles again toward the window, carefully concealing her expression from Masachika while letting out a delighted sound in her heart.

("Idiot! He totally didn't notice! Hehe!") 

Alisa pretends to rest her chin on her hand, suppressing the corners of her mouth that almost curve upward. Masachika gazes at her back with eyes full of pity.

("No, wait—everything got through, didn't it?") 

Alisa has no idea.

In fact, Masachika understands Russian perfectly. Every shy little phrase she murmurs softly in Russian reaches Masachika directly.

And none of the other students around them have any clue that this seemingly bland conversation between the two is actually hiding a rather comical and slightly embarrassing exchange behind the scenes.
